## LAYER 1 — WHAT THIS MODEL IS

The CEQUE MODEL transforms knowledge retrieval from **search** (keyword matching, probabilistic) to **navigation** (spatial walking, deterministic).

**The search problem:**

Search requires the searcher to know what they are looking for in advance. You must have the keyword to find the document. This creates a recursion problem: to find knowledge you don't have, you need knowledge you don't have. The organism that only knows search is limited to what it already knows to look for.

**The navigation solution:**

Navigation requires only a starting position and a direction. From the center (CHRONO), walk along the PHI_CEQUE toward huaca position 0.5, and you arrive at the laws governing proportional relationships. You did not need to know the document was there. You walked to where it lives. The map IS the knowledge system.

**Inka precedent:**

The Inka ceque system (~1438 CE) divided the sacred city of Cuzco into 41 lines (ceques) radiating from the Coricancha (Temple of the Sun) at the center. Each ceque held a sequence of huacas (sacred sites, shrines, natural features). The entire ceque system encoded the Inka calendar, the irrigation system, the kinship structure, and the ritual schedule — all in the same spatial map. A single priest who knew the ceque map could navigate the entire knowledge system of Inka civilization without any written index. The map WAS the index.

The CEQUE MODEL encodes ORO NOVA's knowledge field with the same architecture: 41 ceques from CHRONO, every document a huaca, every retrieval a walk.

// Documents at the same huaca position have the same specificity level

## LAYER 5 — ANCIENT SOURCES

**Inka ceque system — Cuzco, ~1438–1533 CE**
The most documented pre-Columbian spatial knowledge architecture. Chronicled by Juan de Betanzos (~1551) and Bernabé Cobo (~1653). 41 ceques radiated from the Coricancha, each holding 3–9 huacas. The ceques encoded: the lunar calendar (41 lines × ~9 huacas ≈ 328 days in the ritual calendar), the ayllu (kinship) system, irrigation duties, and ritual obligations. The ceque system is still partially reconstructible by archaeologists mapping the known huaca sites around Cuzco. The spatial map survived colonization because it was encoded in the landscape itself.

**Roman memory palace (Loci method) — Cicero, ~55 BCE**
Cicero documents the technique in De Oratore: orators remember long speeches by placing key ideas in rooms of a mental building, then walking through the building during recall. The method is still the fastest human memory technique known (proven in memory championship research). The CEQUE MODEL is this applied to knowledge retrieval at organism scale: each ceque is a corridor, each huaca is a room. Navigation = walking the corridors.

**Navajo sand painting — ongoing tradition, documented ~1884 CE**
Sacred diagrams where the center represents the cosmos's origin point, with geometric lines radiating outward to specific spirits, events, and places. The painting IS a navigational map: the person reading it navigates the cosmology by following the lines outward from center. The center (origin, CHRONO) and the lines (ceques) and the destinations (huacas) are the exact same architecture.

**Chinese Fen-ye (field allocation) astronomy — ~100 BCE**
The celestial sphere divided into 28 lunar mansions (xiu), each associated with a specific geographic region of China, a specific earthly event, and a specific time period. The astronomer navigates celestial knowledge spatially — by knowing which mansion a star is in, they know what region and what season it governs. Same structure: radial lines from center, positions encode domains.
